    Homeschooling with Board Games: Pandemic Edition
      [draft]

    February 19, 2021

    A lot has changed in the last year. I started a new job in April 2020. My 8-year old daughter started homeschooling. I started hoarding wine (who needs toilet paper?). And we play a lot more board games.

    Appropriately, one of those board games is called Pandemic.

    For those unfamiliar with the game, Pandemic is a co-operative game …

    Which city is the most important?#

    me: Which city do you think is the most important in the game?

    daughter: London

    me: Why?

    daughter: Because we live there!

    Before we can answer “which city is the most important”, we need to decide what “importance” means. The city you live in is the most important for your own survival, but which cities are the most important for winning the game? One way to think about this is to re-frame the question as: “how bad is it if there is an outbreak in this city?” Answering this will help players decide which city to prioritise when treating a disease.

    Bogotá is connected to five different cities (Buenos Aires, Sáo Paulo, Miami, Mexico City, and Lima). So an outbreak in Bogotá will result in the yellow infection spreading to five new cities. On the other hand, Santiago is connected to only Lima. So an outbreak in Santiago will lead to only one extra infected city. From this perspective, it feels reasonable to say Bogotá is more important than Santiago.

    In network analysis the number of links connecting to a node (i.e. a city) is called the degree of that node. Bogotá has a degree of 5 because it connects to five other cities, meanwhile Santiago has a degree of 1. The centrality of a node is an indicator of how important the node is within the network. One measure of centrality is the degree centrality, which simply means ranking the nodes by their degree:
